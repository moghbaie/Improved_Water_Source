Improved water source for everyone

"Better progress has been made on improving access to water: 91% of the global population uses an improved water source – one that protects water from external contamination – up from 76% in 1990. The number of people who lack access to an improved source of drinking water has fallen below 700 million people – to 663 million – for the first time." The Guardian

As you see in the following map, there are countries with 40%-50% of population improved water access. Some of them have enough source of water to purify, some have exhausted the source of the water, some located in a very dry area and some a combination of them. The goal of this project is to find those countries that don't have access to any source of the water and need to import drinking water. Also find those country with abundant access to drinking water and could possibly export to the countries in need of the drinking water. Population, baseline water source and drought severity are other factors that would be considered in the calculation. The distance and transportation between countries that could be supplier and consumer is important too.


To investigate the possibility of providing water nationally, baseline water stress was drawn on the map. As you see countries like Afghanistan has 55.3% improved water source and baseline water stress of 4 out of 5 (It's in high range- it shows the % of annual water withdrawal to total water). Obviously Afghanistan needs to import drinking water. Where countries like Chad with 50.80% improved water source has 0.52 baseline water stress( It's low- It means there might be possible source of water inside the country).



Another important factor is population. Countries with higher population and higher population growth rate have more priority. Big countries with good access to improve water source and lower population could be good candidate to export drinking water to more critical countries. Drought severity measure(It measures the average length of drought times the dryness of the droughts from 1901 to 2008- it's between 1 and 5) helps us to eliminate the water supplier countries. A country that has drought severity more than 2 could not be a source of water for other countries.



Finally after joining all the data from worldbank.org and wri.org and careful study, list of potential consumer and supplier of drinking water was made. The output could be use to start a business to provide drinking water to the countries with low access to improved water. Australia could be a good source of the drinking water for countries like new Guinea and south eastern african country like, Madagascar, Mozmbique and Tanzania. Japan could be another country to export drinking water to mongolia. Turkey has strategic position and aproximity to conutries like Afghanistan and golf countries including Saudi. Spain, Italy, Germany and UK could be good source of drinking water for north and west african countries such as Angola. I have to mention that there are not clear data available for many african countries, and some have not been considered in the calculation due to lack of data or discrepancy in the data.
